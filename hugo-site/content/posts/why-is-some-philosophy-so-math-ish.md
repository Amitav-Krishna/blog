Hello!  Quick throwaway post here.  I was reading [Normative Uncertainty](https://static1.squarespace.com/static/5506078de4b02d88372eee4e/t/5bc723b5104c7bf5cc8f3881/1539777748456/MacAskill-Normative-Uncertainty.pdf) by [William MacAskill](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_MacAskill)(Effective Altruism man) when I was flashbanged by the math.  After some chatting with claude, apparently this is what makes analytic philosophy notable, as opposed to continental philosophy which is focused on society, culture, etc.  What they don't learn about apparently is *analysis*, which unlike *logic*, deals with continuous things.
